>If you haven't heard yet please note that there has been well over 20
>break and entries in the neighborhood of Altadore and South Calgary in
>the past 2 months.  I contacted the Calgary Police Service and talked to
>Constable Schwartz from the district #2 office (249-7701) and she
>confirmed that there is a big problem in the area.  Typically these
>burglars have been young (early 20's), male, carrying backpacks and most
>of the break-in's occur during the day.  They have been ringing doors to
>see if someone is home and if not they have been kicking in the doors
>and entering.  The are generally after small items; cash, jewelry, CD's,
>anything that can be quickly sold for cash.  Thankfully, there hasn't
>been too much vandalism as of yet.  Interestingly enough most of these
>people are thought to live within the neighborhood.
>
>The police are well aware of the problem and are working to stop the
>epidemic.  They ask that anyone who sees any suspicious behavior or a
>B&E in progress to call 911 immediately to get it checked out.
>
>They have also noted that there are quite a few scams occurring where
>people will pose as people trying to raise money via the sales of
>chocolate bars.  The people without credible ID are thought to be
>illegitimate.  Keep your eyes open for them as they could be working in
>packs to case the house.  Again, inform the police if you suspect
>suspicious behavior.
>

>from the Calgary Police Service as to the latest developments in the
>area.
